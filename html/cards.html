Holiday Cards

2011 - Present day

No project management, no instructions, no explanations. I cherish the people in my life and at the end of every year, I use that love to fuel me to create a unique greeting card. When the right design emerges, a creative flow ensues and nothing gets questioned until all cards are stamped and sent! 2011

Mounted Linoleum Block Printing

The very first card. Inspired by designing my first "mass market" product, a tote bag, for the Whole Foods Market in Virignia.

2012

Reduction Printing

My most memorable card, as this card is when I commited to continuing the holiday card tradition annually.

2013

Mounted Linoleum Block Printing

Moving to Portland, OR, I had decided to focus on the Pacific Northwests's infamous cryptid: Big Foot.

2014

Linocut Printing

A card that indicated my behavior every year! As much as I try to start planning card in October, time always defeats me and I rush at the end of December.

2015

Multi-block Linocut Printing

A pivotal move in my life's journey, 2015 was when I moved to Texas.

2016

Screen Printing

After a spiritual trip around Iceland, the history of witches inspired this print. Based on the Ace of Cups tarot card, this card represents the emotional world, joy, and contentment.

2017

Multi-block Linocut Printing, Zoetrope

This card unveiled my absolute joy in making a card with absolutely no explanations. This card was sent with the slits removed and the base scored for recipients to pop-out and build a zoetrope.

2018

Anaglyph 3D

Honestly, the inspiration here was just wanting to create an anaglyph image. These cards were mailed with red/blue glasses.

2019

Transparency, glitter

Although clearly a European influence, this Krampus shadow was drawn to look like a shark after a memorable dive swimming with sharks in Australia.

2020

Paper, Lights, Copper Tape

How could I end 2020 without making a Covid themed card? Austin was my backdrop, my home, my rock I lived under for the year.

2021

Paper, Augmented Reality, 3D Animation in Blender

Another challenging year, but I was determined to figure out how to make this idea a reality. This QR code and AR experience is still active! The QR code will open a link and ask for access to your camera, I promise it's only to let the experience work. Keep your camera pointed at the code!
